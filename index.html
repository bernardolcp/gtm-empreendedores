The Critical Point

When every company is a
of technology company, the differentiator is knowing
which technology to build.

STAGE 01
🤯
“Holy sh*t, look at this”
First time using AI. Pure dopamine. Everything feels magical. You want to automate the world.
STAGE 02
🚀
“We are going to dominate”
10 new tools per week. Parallel projects. Lots of energy, little direction. Everything is a priority.
STAGE 03
😵
“Where is the result?”
Tool spend does not create ROI — it creates a feeling of progress. Lots of output, little outcome. The numbers do not move.
STAGE 04
🎯
Operate like tech
Sprints, roadmaps, architecture. Start small, think scalable. Clarity on what will be delivered separates anxiety from progress.
MOST PEOPLE STOP HERE
RESULTS START HERE

If every company is a technology company, operate like one. Scalable systems, security from day one, sprints with clear scope. What separates pathological AI anxiety from real progress is clarity and alignment on what will be delivered.

The Trap

Do not confuse dopamine
with progress.

FUN

  • Creating new things with AI
  • Testing 10 tools per week
  • Automating what nobody asked for
  • Posting “look what AI did”
vs

ROI

  • Eliminating a real P&L bottleneck
  • Reducing cost on one income-statement line
  • Accelerating existing revenue
  • Scaling what already works

Use Case 04 — Superpowers

What you unlock beyond chat.

⏱️
Routines
cloud cron · repo + connectors
Recurring tasks that run by themselves in the cloud — connected to your GitHub repo and MCPs. They update data, generate reports, and monitor campaigns on autopilot.
☁️
Remote / Headless
claude --remote
Control Claude Code from your phone. Send tasks from anywhere — it executes on your computer or in the cloud.
🔄
Loop
/loop 5m check metrics
Runs a task on an automatic interval. Monitors campaigns, checks errors, updates reports — without you asking again.
🤖
Specialist Subagents
agents in parallel
Improves project quality. Each subagent can be trained with specific content — one copy specialist, one data specialist, one design specialist.
🔌
MCP (Integrations)
Pipeboard, Notion, Gmail...
Connects Claude to external tools. It operates your ads manager, calendar, email — directly from the terminal.
🧠
Persistent Memory
CLAUDE.md · memory/ · LLM wiki
Continuously update CLAUDE.md with rules and preferences. For truly structured content (briefings, profiles, history), use an LLM wiki. Every new conversation starts smart.
QUICK DIFFERENCE
Memory — the assistant remembers things about you and the context.
Skill — the assistant learns a specific way of working.
Wiki / file-based CRM — the truly structured content stays stored there.

Extra Use Case — Knowledge

Knowledge shared across teams.

Create a structure where you can talk with your team through the CLI — and tap into your company’s intelligence in natural language.

STEP 01 — REPO
📁
Team repo
Initial repository structure — folders, conventions, CLAUDE.md, writing rules.
STEP 02 — CONNECTORS
🔗
Database
Integration with Supabase, Notion, Drive. The repository reads and writes where the team already works.
STEP 03 — INTERFACE
💬
Human × AI interface
Chat, Slack, terminal. The team asks in natural language — answers come with source and link.
STEP 04 — INGESTION
🤖
Ingestion agents
Routines that monitor sources, extract, normalize, and index. The wiki grows without anyone editing by hand.
